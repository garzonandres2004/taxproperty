## His Exact Title Research Steps (In Order)

### Step 1: Drive the Property First
**Quote:** *"For every single property that we're interested in, we're going to go ahead and drive that property."*

- Physically visit the property
- Assess condition
- Check neighborhood
- **Decision Point:** If it passes visual inspection, proceed to title research

### Step 2: Find the County Recorder Website
**Quote:** *"The easiest way to find that website is always going to be putting in the county name in Google and then simply deed search."*

**Exact Process:**
1. Google: "[County Name] deed search"
2. Look for "Real Property Records"
3. Verify you're on the right site by looking for "Grantor" and "Grantee" fields

**Quote:** *"I've tested this hundreds and hundreds of times and it's actually never ever failed me."*

### Step 3: Understand Grantor vs Grantee
**Quote:** *"Grantor is when someone's selling, Grantee is when someone's buying."*

- **Grantor (left side):** Person SELLING the property
- **Grantee (right side):** Person BUYING the property  
- **Rule:** Search the name on BOTH sides to get complete picture

### Step 4: Search by Owner Name (NOT County Name for resales)
**Quote:** *"If it's a resale, it's owned by the county right now. And if I search the county name, that's going to bring up thousands and thousands of things."*

**For Resale/OTC Properties:**
- Skip searching the county (too many results)
- Look for previous owner in the property details
- Search by that specific owner name
- Get complete chain of title since they owned it

### Step 5: Check for Municipal Liens
**Quote:** *"From state to state and even county to county, municipal liens can be ambiguous. The county is not going to wipe out their own interest."*

**Key Insight:**
- In Texas: Municipal liens generally survive tax deed sale
- In Michigan: Different rules (varies by county)
- **Action:** Must find municipal lien balance and subtract from max bid

### Step 6: Get the Complete Chain of Title
**Quote:** *"We want to get the entire chain of title for this property since they've owned it."*

- Follow ownership back through time
- Look for gaps or breaks (clouds on title)
- Note any unusual transfers

### Step 7: Cross-Reference with GIS/Property Lines
**Quote:** *"Regrid's cool because it's an app on your phone as well as desktop... You can see yourself in proximity to the parcel you're looking at."*

**Tools Used:**
- Regrid (free mobile app)
- LandGlide (alternative, $10-12/month)

**Purpose:**
- Verify property boundaries
- Confirm address matches parcel
- Avoid landlocked properties
- See property lines while driving

**Quote:** *"Think of it like Google Maps with the little blue dot showing exactly where you are... we can see and make sure we're in the right spot."*

### Step 8: Check Property Value and Rehab Budget
**Quote:** *"We're going to figure out how much it's worth, create a rehab budget, and if it passes through all of those steps... then do a title search."*

**Sequence:**
1. Drive property
2. Check ARV (After Repair Value)
3. Estimate rehab costs
4. **Then** do title search (don't waste time on bad deals)

### Step 9: Final Steps - Lawsuit Search
**Quote:** *"The final two steps is to do a title search and then a lawsuit search or lawsuit title search as well."*

**Two Searches:**
1. Title search (ownership/liens)
2. Lawsuit search (litigation against property)

## Deal Killers (Automatic AVOID)

### 1. Landlocked Properties
**Quote:** *"Make sure you're not buying some hunk of junk that is landlocked in the middle of the desert and you can't read or find the property line."*

- No legal access
- Can't reach the property
- **Solution:** Use Regrid/LandGlide to verify access

### 2. Municipal Liens (in some states)
**Quote:** *"In Texas, for the most part, municipal liens are not going to get wiped out."*

- County/city claims survive sale
- Can be thousands of dollars
- **Solution:** Always check, subtract from max bid

### 3. Wrong Property (Address Mismatch)
**Quote:** *"One of the mistakes people make is maybe they buy something and it's just flat out the wrong property."*

- Address doesn't match parcel
- Buying land instead of house
- **Solution:** Cross-reference county maps with Regrid

### 4. Properties That "Don't Make Sense"
**Quote:** *"Never ever ever trust the property location... Does it make sense on something that's worth close to a million dollars that there would only be $2,800 in taxes?"*

- Opening bid too low for property value
- Indicates problems
- **Solution:** Sanity check: Does tax amount match property value?

## Green Lights (Good Signs)

### 1. Property Passes Visual Drive-By
**Quote:** *"This is a piece of land in Baytown, Texas. As you can see, nice piece of land there. Looks great."*

- Physical condition acceptable
- Accessible
- Neighborhood acceptable

### 2. Opening Bid Makes Sense
**Quote:** *"If the minimum bid is one or two years of taxes and it's $28,000, that sounds a lot more likely for something that's got a high ticket price."*

- Tax amount proportional to value
- Reasonable back taxes
- No red flags

### 3. Clear Chain of Title
- No gaps in ownership
- No unusual transfers
- Clean history

### 4. Available Over-the-Counter
**Quote:** *"Previously went to the auction and nobody bid on it... sometimes over the counter can be good deal."*

- No competition
- First come, first serve
- Time to do thorough research

## State-Specific Variations

### Texas (Tax Deed)
- Municipal liens survive sale
- Must subtract from max bid
- One-year redemption period (20% penalty)

### Michigan (Tax Deed)
- Different beast (his words)
- Rules vary significantly by county
- Must research county-specific rules

### Georgia (Judicial In Rem)
- Called "judicial in rem sales" not "over the counter"
- One-year redemption at 20% interest
- Some have no redemption (title in 2-3 weeks)
- Others have 60-day redemption

## Key Principles

### 1. Don't Research Everything
**Quote:** *"If it passes through all of those steps after we go through that process, if we're still interested in it, then do title search."*

- Drive by FIRST
- Check ARV/rehab costs SECOND
- Only then do expensive title research

### 2. Municipal Liens Are Tricky
**Quote:** *"One of the red flags or one of the topics that can be a bit ambiguous at times are municipal liens."*

- Vary by state/county
- Some survive, some don't
- Always check

### 3. Verify Property Boundaries
**Quote:** *"When you're looking at these, you want to make sure that the address lines up with the county maps and the actual property lines."*

- Never trust address alone
- Always cross-reference with GIS
- Use Regrid/LandGlide while driving

### 4. Search Strategy Matters
**Quote:** *"I've tested this hundreds and hundreds of times... If you search for title and you don't search this [deed search], this is always the first way that you want to search."*

- "County name + deed search" is the magic formula
- Never fails to find the recorder website
- Don't get lost in county portals
